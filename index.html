The Six Signals — Bone Calm Event Stack
These are the six recurring patterns that mark an authentic Bone Calm event in The Bone Wolf River. Every powerful scene in the novel contains some combination of these signals. Paste any passage below to see which ones fire.
Signal 01
🔋 Atmosphere Shifts
The environment changes — silence, temperature, smell, pressure, the world going still before the event.
“The plains outside had gone completely silent. That was the worst part.”
Signal 02
🐶 Animal Reacts First
The animal responds before any human in the scene understands what is happening.
“The hackles were up. All of them. From the base of his skull to the middle of his back.”
Signal 03
✨ Calm Enters Character
The Bone Calm descends — a specific quality of still that is not peace. Not numbness. Something ancient.
“Calm to the bone. Not the blankness of a child who doesn’t understand what fire is.”
Signal 04
🔥 Fire / Light Behaves Unnaturally
Fire or light does something it should not — bends toward something, gathers, illuminates a face from inside.
“The fire played off the faces of the dragons and in that light they would seem to disappear.”
Signal 05
◈ Action Happens with Precision
The action that follows the calm is executed with impossible, inhuman precision — fluid, clean, certain.
“She drew it to full extension and held it perfectly still. Something was helping the child.”
Signal 06
👁 Someone Recognizes It After
A witness names what happened — not in wonder but in recognition. They have seen this before, or something like it.
“That’s a war bow,” he said. “She drew a full war bow.”

Character & World Reference
The people at the center of the story. Their roles. Their connections. What they carry.
Anthony “Chase” Singleton
2026 — Father · Modern Protagonist
Husband of Brittany. Brother of Nicole. He doesn’t believe in inherited things — not curses, not gifts, not anything that didn’t come from work. Everything he has, he built. That was the only kind of thing he trusted.

He is about to be asked to believe in something he cannot explain. What he chooses will determine whether Carter survives what’s coming.
Carter Singleton
2026 — The Marked Child
Born October 13. Ancient smoldering brown eyes that look at things the way you look at something you have already decided about. He puts his hands in the flame and pulls them back unmarked.

His first real word was Fire. Said clearly. Calmly. Like something confirmed rather than discovered. He already knows things Anthony doesn’t.
Brittany (Wetherbee) Singleton
2026 — Mother · Bloodline Carrier
Ice blue eyes — cold, still, deep water. Her maiden name is burned into the back of the old oak chair. She found it at a yard sale in the riverfront neighborhood. Paid forty dollars. She knew it was meant for her. She liked that.

She has seen things she hasn’t told Anthony. She is carrying the knowledge quietly. This is what the bloodline looks like when it knows what it is.
Nicole “Stevie” Singleton
2026 — Anthony’s Sister · Researcher · Pattern Tracker
Eight months investigating. Fourteen cultures. Same entity. Different name in every language. Same face. Same burning eyes. Same children.

She found the Fibonacci pattern in the intervals between major bloodline events. She called Anthony at midnight to tell him about it — the same night the package arrived with Carter’s name on it. “It’s killing kids, Chase.” She is inside the pattern and doesn’t know it yet.

About This System
Why it was built, what it does, and how to get one for your book.
Why I Built This
I was writing The Bone Wolf River and realized I kept losing track of things — patterns I had established, threads I had not resolved, ideas I had spoken out loud and forgotten. So I built a system to hold it all.

It started as notes. Then it became tools. Then it became this.
What It Replaces
Notebooks that do not search. Spreadsheets that do not understand story. Generic writing software built for everyone and therefore built for no one.

This app is built around one book’s specific logic — its signals, its characters, its open threads, its voice patterns. That specificity is the point.
Who It’s For
Authors who take their work seriously. Writers with enough material that keeping track of it matters. Anyone building a story world complex enough to need its own system.
